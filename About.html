Our history

HENU: Empowering Eldery Communities, Enriching Lives


HENU, an indigenous NGO founded in 2007, is committed to improving the lives of disadvantaged community members, with a special focus on older persons and orphans. As a member of HelpAge International, HENU has received prestigious awards for its innovative approach and community capacity building. It is a holder of the first Jack Watters Memorial Award for Strengthening Responsiveness to Older People’s and Concerns in Uganda: an Innovative Approach and 2012 Cordaid HIV and AIDS Award in the area of Community capacity building .

Our Mission

Mission


HENU’s mission is to work tirelessly for the cause and care of vulnerable individuals within the community. By empowering older persons and orphans, HENU aims to enhance their quality of life.

Our vision

Vision

HENU envisions a proactive and empowered community that effectively responds to health-related challenges and other social needs.

ways of working

Community Life Competence (CLCP)


HENU uses A strengths based Approach called Community Life Competence
CLCP is a process that aims at promoting community self-reliance by stimulating older persons to appreciate their strengths to respond to their life concerns
The approach focusses on what older persons & communities can do for themselves, with focused government or NGO support, if necessary.

Focus Areas

HENU’s Areas Of Focus


Health including response to covid-19

  • o COVID-19 Response: HENU distributed soap, posho, and sugar to 1620 older persons in 30 villages. Health messages were shared during distribution.
  • o Community Health: Linking informal and formal healthcare services, raising awareness about non-communicable diseases, and screening for hypertension and diabetes

    • Sanitation and hygiene
  • o Promoting personal and environmental hygiene.
  • o Redesigning pit latrines and managing rubbish.

    • Promotion of Physical Activities (exercise)
  • o Promoting active aging and delaying disability through individual and group exercises.

    • Nutrition
  • o Improving well-being through a balanced diet.
  • o Wisdom Centre and day care services for older persons without caregivers.

    • Support to urban old Refugees
  • o Counseling center for both young and older persons.
  • o Skills development in modern technology, crafts, and tailoring.

    • Research and documentation Intergenerational Self-Help Groups (ISHGs)
  • o Combatting loneliness (as lethal as smoking) by fostering connections.
  • o Collaborating with IDI on the Pictures of Ageing Project in Busukuma.
